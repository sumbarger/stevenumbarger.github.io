Steven R. Umbarger

Ph.D. Student in Astronomy & Astrophysics
University of Michigan

I am a Ph.D. student in Astronomy and Astrophysics at the University of Michigan. My research focuses on understanding how galaxies and their star clusters evolve, particularly through galaxy mergers and high-resolution observations.

I work at the intersection of observational astronomy, simulations, and scientific visualization. I use data from the Keck Observatory and the Hubble Space Telescope, alongside FIRE-2 galaxy simulations, to study the structure and evolution of star clusters in nearby galaxies.

In parallel, I am deeply committed to making astronomy more accessible. I develop 3D models, tactile learning tools, and multimedia visualizations to support learners with visual impairments and to broaden participation in astronomy.

Research Snapshot
