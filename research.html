Research

My research focuses on understanding the formation and evolution of star clusters and galaxies through a combination of high-resolution observations, simulations, and visualization techniques.

Keck Adaptive Optics vs. Hubble Imaging of M31 Star Clusters

I compare high-resolution Keck NIRC2 adaptive optics observations with Hubble Space Telescope imaging to study the structure of star clusters in M31. This work examines how different observing techniques affect measurements of cluster morphology and resolution.

I use image processing tools such as DS9 and 3D visualization software to analyze and compare cluster properties across datasets.
